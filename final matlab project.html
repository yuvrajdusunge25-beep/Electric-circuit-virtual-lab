Aim of the Experiment

This virtual laboratory experiment is designed to help students understand the practical application of linear differential equations in electrical circuit analysis.

Specific Objectives:

Apply Linear Differential Equations to analyze RL and RC circuits

Calculate Current Growth in LR circuits over time

Analyze Charge Accumulation in RC circuits

Investigate Voltage Effects on circuit response

Comprehensive Theory: RL & RC Circuits

Fundamental Concepts

First-Order Linear Differential Equations

The standard form of a first-order linear differential equation is:

dy/dt + P(t)y = Q(t)

Where the solution is obtained using the Integrating Factor Method:

IF = e^(∫P(t) dt), y · IF = ∫Q(t) · IF dt

Circuit Elements and Their Properties

Component Symbol Unit Voltage-Current Relation Energy Storage
Resistor (R) R Ohm (Ω) V = IR (Ohm's Law) Dissipates energy as heat
Inductor (L) L Henry (H) V = L(di/dt) Stores energy in magnetic field
Capacitor (C) C Farad (F) I = C(dV/dt) Stores energy in electric field

RL Series Circuit Analysis

Circuit Diagram: Series connection of Voltage Source (E), Resistor (R), and Inductor (L)

Derivation of Governing Equation

1 Apply KVL to the RL circuit:
E(t) - iR - L(di/dt) = 0
2 Rearrange to standard form:
di/dt + (R/L)i = E(t)/L
3 Identify P(t) and Q(t):
P(t) = R/L, Q(t) = E(t)/L
4 Calculate Integrating Factor (IF):
IF = e^(∫(R/L) dt) = e^((R/L)t)
5 Solve for current i(t):
i(t) = (E/R)(1 - e^(-(R/L)t))

Key RL Circuit Formulas

  • Time Constant: τ = L/R (seconds)
  • Steady-State Current: i_ss = E/R
  • Current at time t: i(t) = i_ss(1 - e^(-t/τ))
  • Voltage across Inductor: V_L(t) = Ee^(-t/τ)

RC Series Circuit Analysis

Circuit Diagram: Series connection of Voltage Source (E), Resistor (R), and Capacitor (C)

Derivation of Governing Equation

1 Apply KVL to the RC circuit:
E(t) - iR - q/C = 0
2 Use i = dq/dt and rearrange:
dq/dt + (1/RC)q = E(t)/R
3 Solve for charge q(t):
q(t) = CE(1 - e^(-t/(RC)))

Key RC Circuit Formulas

  • Time Constant: τ = RC (seconds)
  • Maximum Charge: q_max = CE
  • Charge at time t: q(t) = q_max(1 - e^(-t/τ))
  • Voltage across Capacitor: V_C(t) = E(1 - e^(-t/τ))

Practical Applications

Real-World Applications

  • RL Circuits: Transformers, motors, relays, power transmission lines
  • RC Circuits: Timing circuits, filters, power supplies, signal processing
